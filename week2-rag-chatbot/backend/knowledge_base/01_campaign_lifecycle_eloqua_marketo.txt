Campaign Lifecycle in Oracle Eloqua and Adobe Marketo Engage
Overview
In both Oracle Eloqua and Adobe Marketo Engage, the campaign lifecycle spans planning, creation, scheduling, deployment, and maintenance, but the platforms structure that lifecycle differently. Eloqua emphasizes campaign orchestration through the Campaign Canvas and audience evaluation rules, while Marketo organizes initiatives through programs and automation logic inside Smart Campaigns.
Campaign lifecycle in Oracle Eloqua

Planning
In Eloqua, planning starts with defining the audience, required assets, and whether the campaign should run as a simpler send or as a more advanced multi-step campaign. Audience planning is especially important because the Segment Members element determines how contacts enter the campaign.

Creation
Creation happens by assembling the workflow on the campaign structure, combining audience sources, campaign steps, and communication assets. This allows Eloqua campaigns to support both direct execution and more elaborate nurture journeys.

Scheduling
Scheduling in Eloqua includes campaign activation timing and segment re-evaluation frequency. Oracle states that segment members can be added once at activation or regularly until the campaign is deactivated, with a default re-evaluation frequency of every one day.

Deployment
Deployment begins when the campaign is activated and contacts start entering through the configured audience element. From there, contacts proceed through the configured workflow and asset interactions defined in the campaign.

Maintenance
Maintenance includes monitoring entries, exits, delivery, and stalled records through operational reporting. Oracle provides reports such as Campaign Audit, Campaign Entry, Campaign Exit, Campaign Email Statistics, Campaign SMS Statistics, and Stuck Records Report to support campaign monitoring.

Campaign lifecycle in Adobe Marketo Engage

Planning
In Marketo, planning starts by selecting the appropriate program type: Event, Engagement, Email, or Default. This choice shapes the overall campaign model, whether the initiative is a one-off send, nurture stream, event workflow, or flexible operational program.

Creation
Creation takes place inside programs, which act as containers for local assets such as emails, landing pages, and Smart Campaigns. Campaign logic is then built through Smart Campaigns, where the Smart List defines who qualifies and the Flow defines what happens next.

Scheduling
Scheduling depends on campaign type and automation logic. Batch campaigns are scheduled to run at a set time, while trigger campaigns remain active and process people as qualifying actions occur.

Deployment
Deployment happens when the program or Smart Campaign is activated. Once active, qualified people are processed through the campaign rules configured in the Smart List, Flow, and Schedule structure.

Maintenance
Maintenance requires ongoing review of audience logic, campaign activity, and database hygiene. Because Smart Lists can be dynamic and campaign logic often depends on data quality, poor governance can reduce targeting accuracy and reporting reliability.
