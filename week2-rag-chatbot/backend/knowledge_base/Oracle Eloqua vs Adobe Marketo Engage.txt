# Oracle Eloqua vs Adobe Marketo Engage

## Platform comparison
The two platforms both support enterprise email marketing automation, but they differ in how campaigns are structured and managed.[page:2][web:6] Eloqua centers more visibly on the Campaign Canvas for journey orchestration, while Marketo organizes work through programs powered by Smart Campaigns.[page:2][web:6]

## Comparison table

| Area | Oracle Eloqua | Adobe Marketo Engage |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign types | Uses simple campaigns, A/B testing campaigns, and multi-step campaigns on Campaign Canvas.[page:1] | Uses Email, Engagement, Event, and Default programs.[web:6] |
| Workflow builder | Campaign Canvas is a visual multi-step workflow for orchestrated journeys.[web:1][page:2] | Smart Campaigns drive automation through triggers, filters, and flow steps within programs.[web:9][web:14] |
| Core assets | Emails, SMS, forms, landing pages, and external assets/activities for reporting.[web:2][page:2] | Emails, landing pages, Smart Campaigns, Smart Lists, forms, tokens, and webhooks.[web:6][web:11][web:14] |
| Personalization | Dynamic content, conditional routing, dynamic signatures, and send-time optimization.[web:3][page:2] | Smart Lists, segment filters, tokens, snippets, and program-level personalization patterns.[web:14][web:15][web:19] |
| Reporting | Operational reports, Insight reports, dashboards, campaign metrics, and external activity visibility.[web:2][page:1] | Campaign Activity reports, engagement reporting, and attribution-oriented analysis environments such as Revenue Explorer depending on setup.[web:9][web:17] |
| Strengths | Enterprise scalability, sophisticated orchestration, and broad reporting support.[web:2][page:1] | High flexibility, reusable logic, strong tokenization, and extensibility through webhooks.[web:11][web:14] |
| Challenges | Greater complexity, steeper learning curve, and heavier governance needs.[web:3][page:1] | Requires careful database hygiene and governance for reliable automation and reporting.[web:9][web:15] |

## Practical interpretation
Eloqua is often a stronger fit when an organization needs highly structured, multi-step orchestration and centralized visibility across complex campaigns.[web:3][page:1] Marketo is often a stronger fit when teams prioritize adaptable program templates, tokenized reuse, and flexible execution patterns tied closely to database operations.[web:6][web:14]
